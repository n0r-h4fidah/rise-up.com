THE GOOD SIDE OF STRESS

Eustress is a term coined by Hans Selye, a person who played a major role in stress research, to describe a positive form of stress. Eustress refers to stress that is beneficial and can improve a person's work performance, motivation, and character development.

Eustress happens when people are faced with tense, upbeat or exciting situations that they view as applicable or manageable and potentially rewarding. Most of the time, it is mixed with feelings of excitement, enthusiasm, and a sense of fulfillment. Eustress can arise from many aspects of life, such as work, relationships, personal goals, and physical activities.

The main characteristic of eustress is that it is conceptualized as positive and stimulating rather than overwhelming or hurtful. It gives people the sense of purpose, engagement, and accomplishment. Eustress can help individuals outweigh their limits and escape their comfort zones and inspire them to strive for a better character and achievement.

Eustress can have several benefits:

  1. Increased motivation: Eustress can serve as a powerful motivator, driving individuals to take on challenges and achieve their goals. It can enhance focus, determination, and perseverance.
  2. Improved performance: Eustress can enhance cognitive functioning and performance. It can sharpen attention, improve memory, and increase creativity, leading to better problem-solving and decision-making abilities.
  3. Enhanced resilience: Experiencing and successfully managing eustress can build resilience and the ability to cope with future stressors. It helps individuals develop adaptive strategies and learn from challenging experiences.
  4. Personal growth: Eustress can facilitate personal growth and self-improvement. It encourages individuals to step outside their comfort zones, explore new opportunities, and develop new skills and competencies.

Examples of eustress include starting a new job or project, preparing for a competition or performance, pursuing a challenging academic degree, or engaging in thrilling recreational activities. It is important to note that what may be perceived as eustress for one person can be distress for another, as individual perceptions and coping abilities vary.

Overall, eustress highlights the positive aspects of stress and emphasizes its potential to contribute to personal development, achievement, and well-being. By embracing and effectively managing eustress, individuals can harness its benefits and thrive in the face of challenging situations.
