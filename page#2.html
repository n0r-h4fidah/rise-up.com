Types of Stress

  1. Mental stress - Mental stress is the body's reaction to unfamiliar feelings, circumstances, and happenstances that occur internally and externally throughout our body, resulting in physical, emotional, and cognitive responses. Practicing your capacity in stress management can help you respond with peculiarities in a better process.
  2. Physical stress - Physical stress correlates to the symptoms that come up in the physical body because of the ongoing activation of the stress response. It can cause symptoms such as body aches, fatigue, headaches, increased blood pressure, and more.
  3. Psychological stress - Psychological stress is one natural response to everyday pressures. It can cause symptoms in a person's cognitive or thinking capacity, emotions, and behavior. Overexposing to stress can lead to disorders and hurt a person's normal way of living and responding to his surroundings.
  4. Behavioral stress - Behavioral stress refers to the changes in behavior that happen because of stress. It can result in difference in eating, sleeping, consumption of alcohol, and other bad and unhealthy habits.
  5. Emotional stress - Emotional stress can cause symptoms such as anxiety, irritability, sadness, and other emotional reactions. Fatal stress can result to health disorders such as a person undergoing depression and anxiety.
